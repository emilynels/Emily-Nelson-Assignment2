What is Three Colors?

The Three Colors Trilogy (Trois Couleurs) is a french film trilogy made from 1993 to 1994, directed Krzysztof Kieślowski. The three films in this trilogy are Three Colors: Blue, Three Colors: White, and Three Colors: Red. Together these three films are called the Three Colors Trilogy. Each film was shot in a different country. France, Poland, and Switzerland respectively. The films in the Three Colors Trilogy is classified as psychological drama films. Each film is a narrative piece that follows one main character throughout their journey, all three dealing with the importance of connection. All three films are in French dialogue.

Each film is meant to represent one color of the French flag, and with that each ideal that the color represents. The color blue represents liberty, white represents equality, and red represents fraternity. So each film is based upon it's corresponding ideal.
