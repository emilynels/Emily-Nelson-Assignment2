Three Colors Trilogy

Directed by: Krzysztof Kieślowski
1993-1994

What is Three Colors?

The Three Colors trilogy (French: Trois couleurs, Polish: Trzy kolory) is the collective title of three psychological drama films directed by Krzysztof Kieślowski: Three Colors: Blue (1993), Three Colors: White (1994), and Three Colors: Red (1994). The trilogy was a co-production between France, Poland and Switzerland, in French language, with the exception of White in Polish and French. All three films were co-written by Kieślowski and Krzysztof Piesiewicz (with story consultants Agnieszka Holland and Sławomir Idziak), produced by Marin Karmitz and composed by Zbigniew Preisner.

Each film is meant to represent one color of the French flag, and with that each ideal that the color represents. The color blue represents liberty, white represents equality, and red represents fraternity. So each film is based upon each corresponding ideal.
